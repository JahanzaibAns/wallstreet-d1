Trademark (Frequently Asked Questions)

In order to better facilitate your Trademark inquiries we have prepared general Frequently Asked Questions that will help you understand the Trademark process.

A trademark is a symbol, word, phrase, audio, or design that distinguishes and identifies the source of goods or services in the marketplace. It can be a brand name, logo, slogan, or any distinctive sign.

Registering a trademark provides legal protection for your brand, preventing others from using the same or similar marks. It also helps in building brand recognition and trust with customers. If someone infringes on my trademark you have the legal right to take action against the infringing Party. This may involve sending cease and desist letters, negotiating, or pursuing legal action through the courts.

Trademarks protect brands and logos, copyrights protect original creative works (like books, music, and art), and patents protect new inventions or processes.

You can order a comprehensive trademark search report through FilingDesks or any other relevant trademark agency. You can trademark names or logos that contain common words if they are used in a unique way to distinguish your goods or services from others.

A trademark registration can last indefinitely as long as you continue to use the mark in commerce and file the required maintenance documents.
